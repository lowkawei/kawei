Will Australia save the super heroes?

The rumour that JJ Abrams (director of TV’s Felicity, Alias and Lost) wanted to make the new Superman’s arch nemesis Lex Luthor as super-powerful as the titular character had many fans up in arms. It may have been why he was dropped from the project. Now, initial dailies from Bryan Singer’s (X-Men, X-Men 2) Superman Returns, currently filming in Sydney, have been given the thumbs up by the comic book nerds at Comics2film web site.

And although the script for Mark Steven Johnson’s (Daredevil) Melbourne-filmed Ghost Rider has been lamented by some fans, most claim it is ‘very fun’. Further good news for comic book fans is that the upcoming Wonder Woman project, also likely to be shot in Australia, will be written and directed by Joss Whedon of TV’s Buffy: The Vampire Slayer, a man well versed in portraying ‘arch-nemesises... ses’. It appears that the world of silver screen comic book heroes is safe – for now.
