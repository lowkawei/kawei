Talkin’ short film in Melbourne

From engineer to sculptor, Thomas Baricevic has finally found his calling in short film, as the director of one of Melbourne’s most popular, regular short film evenings: Fitzroy Shorts. On the first Wednesday of the month, Tom and his crew turn the Night Cat into the place to be. Pauline de Laveaux has a chat with the curator to see how it all came about.

Q. Hi Tom. Welcome. Thanks very much for taking the time to have a chat. You’re the man when it comes to a regular short film event in Melbourne, how long have you been involved with short films?

I’ve been making short films since ’96 and have been screening short films since mid ‘99. The first incarnation of the monthly short film event that I ran with two others was a night called Short Trips and it ran 'til the end of 2003. We began screening monthly at the Tramway Hotel in North Fitzroy and then left to screen at Monties Bar on Smith St.

Q. What was the inspiration to start Fitzroy Shorts?

I suppose since all the members of Short Trips went their own way at the end of 2003, I decided to move to a better venue – The Night Cat – and began it in 2004 with my partner-in-crime Anna Reeves.

Q. Where do you see FS in five years time?

I would like to think that we’ll still be around. Fitzroy Shorts, believe it or not, is still in its embryonic phase and will probably go through some metamorphosis in the coming years. I don’t mind the prospect of the event evolving or even merging with another event. I’d still like to retain the name Fitzroy Shorts, because it gives the people of Fitzroy an identity with a cultural event. >>>
