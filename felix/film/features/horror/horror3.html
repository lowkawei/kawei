Cabin Fever provides the laughs, whilst you’re screaming!

‘definitely a bit of nudity, you know…some sorority girl action.’

One of the more forthright responses I came across when asking what people liked in horror films was, ‘definitely a bit of nudity, you know…some sorority girl action.’ Yeah, I know, it’s a recurring theme.

Wes Craven had one of his teens say it in Scream: ‘here comes the obligatory tit shot!’ Eli Roth’s directorial debut, Cabin Fever, will most certainly satisfy those viewers who like a bit of that style of action and it will have you screaming and laughing at the same time.

It provides many female victims, but the guys don’t escape either. The set-up is far from original; five horny college students take a trip to an old log cabin where they’ll have sex and party, but something comes out of the woods to disrupt their fun and shatter their lives! No, it’s not a bloodthirsty maniac or mutated monster, but an infectious flesh-eating virus that has contaminated the water. Try shaving you’re legs in the bath tub, he, he, he.

The film is hilariously grotesque, and constantly parodies itself as Roth pays a debt to such directors as Tobe Hooper, George A. Romero and Sam Raimi. Most horror films these days reference a few of their predecessors from the 60s, 70s and 80s, and why not pay homage to those who paved the bloodstained paths? Cabin Fever does just that, and it does it well. 

In the end, if you want to satisfy your lust for horror, I'd suggest watching the oldies first, then you’ll have all the fun picking up all the cues from the classics and citing references always makes a film more satisfying.
