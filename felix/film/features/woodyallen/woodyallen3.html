Woody Allen with some of the Melinda and Melinda cast. Photo courtesy of Fox Searchlight Pictures.

‘I'd love to sometime try a picture where I was a winner.’

Please be upstanding

Speaking of nuances, Allen began his career as a stand-up comedian, but he’s not about to follow Robin Williams’ lead by getting back on stage. ‘I miss doing stand-up, but I'm too lazy to do it again,’ he says, smilingly. ‘To write an act, to be the funny stand-up for 45 minutes is a huge amount of work, more work than a movie.’

Allen’s world remains the movies, and his work speaks volumes when it comes to the history of American cinema. A true auteur in the classic tradition, Allen says that even at this point in his career (he’s pushing 70), he still has much to do and tell.

‘I've made romantic films and common films,’ he says, ‘but I would like to see if I could come up with something that was bolder and more aggressive. I've always been a passive comedian, in the mould of Bob Hope or someone that's victimised – a coward, a failure with women, a loser – and I'd love to sometime try a picture where I was a winner. I would like that just for the fun of it. When you see a Groucho Marx or a WC Fields, they have an aggressive sense of humour, and I'd love to try that. Now, it might not sit well, they might say “who is this guy with the confidence?”, but I would like to try that?’

Woody Allen, aggressive womaniser? An interesting prospect indeed.
