Discover Melbourne’s live music scene

You’ve heard Melbourne’s got a great live music scene but have no idea how to tap into it? Let Daniel Corbett give you some directions.

On any given night in Melbourne there is a diverse range of live music on offer. Making a decision about where to go and what to see can be overwhelming. Here are a few simple steps to set you on the path to a stellar live music experience:

Grab a street press

Melbourne has two major street presses, Beat and Inpress. Truth be told, they’re pretty similar. Both are free, released on Wednesdays and have news, reviews, interviews and lots of ads (they’ve gotta make money somehow). You can pick them up from most music stores, plenty of pubs and some clothing stores.

Both street presses have a few pages worth checking out:

  • the gig guides list the week’s shows
  • the music news sections contain press releases from local and touring bands
  • the live music reviews describe various bands playing in Melbourne

Once you’ve chosen a band, Inpress lists the addresses of most local venues to help you track them down. Or you can use our helpful list of Melbourne music venues.

The Age and the Herald Sun newspapers have entertainment pages on Fridays and the weekends, but list fewer gigs.

Get online

If you want to peruse the scene online, you can find current information about bands and gigs at Faster Louder. To find out what the local music community are listening to, check out Mess and Noise. The discussion area’s packed with gossip and tips about the latest bands to hit the scene. Unfortunately, it also tends to be populated by groupies and scenesters who can be judgemental and, well, bitchy. Other general information web sites include MelbournePubs.com and Citysearch.

Radio research

But who wants to read about music anyway? Melbourne is blessed with independent radio stations that support the local scene. Two of the oldest and most popular are Triple R and PBS FM. Both celebrate a variety of musical tastes – just tune into the shows you like best. The DJs plug gigs at the drop of a hat and are a friendly bunch. If you hear a song you like, call the station and ask if the band has any gigs coming up.

Musical chats

This is the key to finding your place in the Melbourne music scene – hearing something you like and talking about it. If radio’s not your thing, hit the streets and go to some music stores.

There are heaps of independent stores in the city and inner suburbs; many staffed by Hi-Fidelity types, happy and eager to chat about bands and gigs. Sure they’ll probably try to sell you albums. But in any half-decent store you can try before you buy. A lot of these joints, like Gaslight, Polyester and Missing Link, also double as ticket outlets for gigs. You might even catch a band previewing their album live in-store. >>>
