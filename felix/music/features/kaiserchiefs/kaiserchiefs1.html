I Predict A Riot — interview with Simon Rix from Kaiser Chiefs

Kaiser Chiefs are four lads from Leeds storming the music world with their fresh take on Britpop. Jaymz Clements asks Kaiser Chiefs’ bass player why they write songs about Volkswagens instead of Chevys.

In the UK it’s raining bands – incredibly talented bands. A distinct new sound is emerging, influenced by some of the seminal British groups of the 80s and 90s; bands such as The Clash, New Order, The Smiths, XTC, The Cure and Joy Division. Enter Kaiser Chiefs – brimming with British flavour – and the UK can’t get enough of them.

Kaiser Chiefs are currently climbing up the UK chart with two hit singles in their pocket – I Predict A Riot and Oh My God. On the verge of international success, the band has the British music press salivating. But they are trying to not get carried away with the hype.

I spoke to Simon Rix, Kaiser Chiefs’ bass player, about the band’s newfound success. When I call he has just woken up. ‘We’ve all just got up,’ he sleepily croaks in his thick Leeds burr. ‘We’re just on a UK tour at the moment; we’re about halfway through. We’re at Cambridge, which is nice. I’ve never been here before.’

The Kaiser Chiefs are on tour because they’ve just released their debut album, Employment. Rix says the title evolved because the members of Kaiser Chiefs ‘were all working crap jobs in Leeds’ when they wrote the album. Employment channels the spirit of The Clash, XTC, Television and latter day heroes such as Blur. Quite simply, it’s Britpop as all fuck. And it’s good. >>>
