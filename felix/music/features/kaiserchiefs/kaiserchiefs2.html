Interview with Simon Rix from Kaiser Chiefs

Q. What’s it like to have a hit album out? You guys must be ecstatic?

Yeah, it feels very good. It’s still in the Top 10 here in the UK. People seem to be buying it and saying it’s good. It’s one thing for friends and family to buy the album out of obligation. It’s another when they say they actually like it. Everyone seems to like it; it’s a relief.

Q. Two producers worked on your debut album Employment; Stephen Street (Blur, The Smiths, New Order) and Steve Harris (U2). What were they like to work with?

It was good working with both of them. We used two producers because we had limited time and Stephen Street was doing New Order as well. Stephen Street was a really easy man to work with. In fact, although we rushed to finish Employment, both producers were great to work with.

Q. But you guys must have felt a little intimidated working with such ‘hot’ producers?

Not really, because they’re really nice guys. Especially Steve Harris, he just stuck us in a room and let us play. Stephen Street was kind of clinical and precise about things, but they were equally good. They weren’t intimidating in anyway. Plus they wanted to work with us, which was cool.

Q. In just 18 months Kaiser Chiefs have achieved local success and international recognition. How have you boys achieved so much in such a short time?

The first year was quite hard, and we were really pro-active and found a bunch of good people to work with. It took us a while to get signed in the first place, maybe a year. And since then, it’s really been quite hectic.

Q. It’s incredibly difficult to crack the US market; even Robbie Williams can’t get a look in! What was it like to break into the US?

A lot of the struggle to attain success in the US – like getting airplay – our management took care of for us. We’ve been over there a couple of times for gigs and things and we’ve seen the result of it. When I got told we had been played on KROQ in LA, I was like 'Erm, well, good.' But that was what started all the US interest. Touring with Franz Ferdinand also helped. We actually played with Franz and Graham Coxon at Royal Albert Hall the other day, and that was very nice. So it’s all going well really. >>>
