Kasier Chiefs hit the headlines with their distinct British sound. Photo courtesy of Live UK

Trivia

The band formed in Leeds in 2003.

Kaiser Chiefs consists of Andrew, Nick, Ricky, Luke and Simon.

Simon, Nick and Ricky were all in the same class at school.

Andrew is an ex-BMX champion.

Their first three-track demo was recorded, mixed and mastered by the band in Nick’s flat.

Their album Employment went platinum in the UK in May.

Nick and Ricky also run a club night in Leeds called ‘Pigs’.

Interview with Simon Rix from Kaiser Chiefs

Q. Kaiser Chiefs are a breath of Britpop fresh air. Have you made a conscious effort to sound British?

When we formed, there were a lot of Strokes, White Stripes, Jet, and the Hives kind of bands in the charts. We thought we weren't going to get signed because bands like that were popular. So, as a reaction to that, we decided to be overtly British in our sound, we went more towards that kind of thing.

Q. So you wanted to write about being British?

It’s not writing about being British, it’s just writing about what you know. Things you see on a day-to-day basis, girls that we’ve known, and things like that. So most lyrics are Leeds-based really. You get other bands talking about American things like, I don’t know, driving Chevys and stuff like that. We don’t have any experience with Chevys so we write about, I don’t know, driving Volkswagens. That’s what we know.

Q. The pressure to achieve greatness can overwhelm a band when they are labelled the ‘Next Big Thing’. Are you concerned about what lies ahead?

We’re not really worried about success, like being famous and stuff. Our aim has always been to put out an album that you can pick up in ten years, listen to again and think it's still a good album. Just to be a group of that kind of note, that’s our ambition.

Q. So you feel confident about the band’s future?

Yeah. We think we’re really good, and we’ve been playing the biggest shows we’ve ever played in the UK in the last couple of weeks. It’s been really exciting. We’re booking bigger ones for autumn, and are just out there enjoying it, and trying to write an album or two really.

Q. With hype surrounding you left, right and centre, will the Chiefs grace Australia with their presence?

I think we’re definitely coming in autumn. Basically, we’re on tour for the rest of the year; England, Europe, America, then back for the UK and European festivals, some dates with U2 in Europe, then back to America, another UK tour, and then over to Australia. It should be pretty cool. Lots of stamps in the passport.
