How to find and buy rare music online

Buying rare music online can be complicated. Use our helpful tips to prepare for your purchase, locate music and then buy your most sought after music items.

Prepare

  • Know your goal. Do you want an original, or will a reissue suffice? Is it the physical album you covet, or the music itself? (The music might be readily available on a compilation or as an MP3) All Music has a massive database. Search by artist or recording to get an idea of what’s available.
  • Set a budget. Know your financial limits before you begin your search. Avoid getting sucked in by claims of rarity or the competitive nature of online bidding.
  • Consider the quality. Unless an item is advertised as mint, it’s probably going to have some wear. Records are especially prone to damage – CDs are a safer online buy. Serious collectors should consider how the current condition will impact an album’s future value.

Locate

  • Start with superstores. Go to sites like eBay and Amazon first. You’ll determine basic selling prices and see how available your coveted album is.
  • Progress to specialists. Music-only sites like GEMM and MusicStack might have more elusive items. These two sites are almost identical and major rare-music marketplaces. Register and create a ‘wants’ list that vendors can see, and the music may even find you.
  • Search small shops. If you can’t find what you want, use a search engine to find small stores around the world that might stock your record. New York’s Sundazed Music reissues long-lost rock albums on vinyl and CD, and vinyl specialists Vintage Vinyl and Aquarius Records catalogue their rarities online.
  • Ask for help. If the album still eludes you, contact other rare music fans. Sites like Vinyl Vulture and Tigersushi are dedicated to rare recordings. Both sites have forums where you can post your wish list and get tips from other collectors.
  • Don’t get discouraged. If you don’t find what you want, keep trying. Online sellers’ catalogues can change every minute – what is missing today might be available tomorrow.

Buy

  • Know the supplier. Most large sites connect you with an individual vendor. GEMM, MusicStack, eBay and Amazon rate vendors and post previous customer’s comments to inform prospective buyers. If negative comments dominate, beware.
  • Watch the auctions. On eBay you bid against others – check the site regularly to avoid being outbid at the last minute. Bidding wars cause inflation, compare prices before committing to expensive items.
  • Do the math. Calculate the total cost of your purchase. Convert foreign prices to your own currency, add shipping costs and taxes to avoid a shock when your credit card bill arrives.
