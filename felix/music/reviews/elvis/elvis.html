Elvis Presley
Love, Elvis
BMG

Aaaahhhh. We can rest easy now because this collection allows everyone to hear Are You Lonesome Tonight? on the 500th Elvis compilation. Save for those who have been living in some cryogenic chamber for the past 50 years, Always On My Mind, Love Me Tender, Fever and 20 others will hold nothing new. Unless you regard For The Good Times, an alternate take, as reasonable cause to purchase this record.

BMG executives must be laughing all the way to the mint. Every time a new artist loses too much money, they have the solution: release another collection by The King. No thought needs to be applied to this process. Just plonk some chestnuts together with a new title and new cover, and start drowning in money.

Yes, it’s easy to be cynical and question the reason behind Love, Elvis. Nonetheless, the world abounds with Elvis fanatics who will marvel at the latest wiz-bang technology used to remaster these songs – that are, without doubt, legendary. They deserve their elevated position in the corpus of popular music. Songs such as Surrender, There Goes My Everything, Always On My Mind – all recorded before The King became Donut King – still resonate with a certain spark. If that’s not enough, some fans may even get all hot and bothered by the cover, which captures Elvis about to engage in some serious tongue action with a young vixen. Each to their own.

For Elvis completists only, although his voice still manages to tear your heart out when it wants to.

Bronius Zumeris
