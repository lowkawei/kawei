Love as Laughter
Laughter’s Fifth
Sub Pop

I couldn’t believe that this melange of Pavement and the early seventies Stones was the same Love as Laughter whose first album, The Greks Bring Gifts, I like for totally different reasons.

This album starts off on two very strange tangents. Firstly, a pumpin’ instant-classic called In Amber with one of those irresistible ascending rock basslines. This song debts to a famous Stereolab song I can’t remember and, probably, stuff like The Fall’s This Nation’s Saving Grace. To be honest if the rest of the album was trash this song would make it worthwhile. Then secondly, a West Coast Experimental Pop Art Band cover, I Won’t Hurt You (you know, the one that sounds a little like Peter Paul and Mary’s Lemon Tree) done very respectfully.

The rest of the album is off on that wild honky tonkin’ free radicalism, like a tasteful Aerosmith or perhaps a less tasteful version of the early period Angels, with the ghost of Stephen Malkmus always kind of hovering in the doorway. Cover art is diabolical, but probably meant to be.

David Nichols
